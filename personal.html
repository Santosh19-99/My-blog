Aarati’s Solo Journey

Aarati had spent years fulfilling responsibilities—family, work, endless routines. But deep inside, she longed for something more. One evening, on a whim, she booked a solo trip to Rara Lake. The journey wasn’t easy. The bumpy roads, the exhausting trek, and the self-doubt made her wonder if she had made a mistake. But when she finally arrived, all the fatigue melted away. Before her stretched the deep blue waters of Rara, cradled by misty hills. The silence was different—not empty, but peaceful. Sitting by the lake, she realized how much she had neglected herself in the rush of life. She dipped her fingers into the icy water, feeling a strange sense of clarity. This journey wasn’t just about travel—it was about rediscovering herself. As she hiked back the next day, tired but fulfilled, she smiled. She had taken the first step toward something new, and that was enough.

TRAVEL DIARIES

Lost in the Streets of Kyoto

Raj had always dreamed of visiting Japan. When he finally stepped onto the ancient streets of Kyoto, he felt like he had entered a different world—where the past and present coexisted in perfect harmony. The wooden machiya houses, the scent of fresh matcha, and the distant chime of temple bells filled him with excitement. Determined to explore beyond the usual tourist paths, Raj wandered through narrow alleyways, following the sound of a distant flute. Without realizing it, he had lost track of time—and his way. His phone had no signal, and the signs were all in Japanese. Just as panic began to creep in, an elderly woman approached him with a kind smile. She didn’t speak English, but she gestured for him to follow. She led him through winding streets, past hidden gardens and tiny shrines, until they arrived at a small teahouse. Inside, she poured him a cup of warm matcha and patted his shoulder as if to say, “You’re safe.” With a grateful heart, Raj sipped the tea, realizing that sometimes, getting lost is the best way to truly find a place—and yourself

A Night in Mustang

Prakash had always wanted to visit Mustang, a place he had only seen in photographs—where the Himalayas met the desert, and the wind whispered ancient stories. When he finally arrived in Lo Manthang, the last forbidden kingdom of Nepal, he felt like he had stepped into another era. The whitewashed monasteries, prayer flags fluttering in the wind, and the endless golden hills mesmerized him. As night fell, he sat outside a small teahouse, sipping butter tea while listening to the stories of an old local man. The sky above was unlike anything he had ever seen—millions of stars shining brightly, untouched by city lights. Just then, a gust of Mustang’s famous wind howled through the valley. The old man chuckled, “This wind carries the voices of our ancestors.” Prakash smiled, realizing that some places weren’t just meant to be visited—they were meant to be felt.

Lost in Bhaktapur

Aashish had always been fascinated by Nepal’s ancient cities, and Bhaktapur was at the top of his list. One afternoon, he wandered through its labyrinth of brick alleys, admiring the intricately carved wooden windows and the scent of freshly made juju dhau drifting through the air. He was so lost in the beauty of the place that he didn't realize he had strayed far from the main square. The familiar sight of Nyatapola Temple was gone, replaced by narrow alleys lined with old houses and hidden courtyards. He checked his phone—no signal. As he stood there, unsure of where to go, a little girl playing nearby noticed his confusion. With a bright smile, she pointed toward a narrow street and said, “Dattatreya Temple!” He followed her direction and soon found himself in front of the ancient temple, where locals sat chatting and an artist was carefully painting a mandala. Aashish laughed at himself—sometimes, getting lost was just another way of discovering something new.
